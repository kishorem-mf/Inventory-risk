# Master Whitepaper: Inventory Imbalance Scenarios in Supply Chain  

## Context  
Inventory imbalances in supply chains can manifest as **understock (shortages)** or **overstock (excess inventory)**.  
- **Understock** â†’ Projected stock falls below safety stock or negative, leading to potential stockouts and service-level issues.  
- **Overstock** â†’ Projected stock consistently above inventory targets, causing high carrying costs, working capital lock-in, and obsolescence risk.  

Both conditions can arise from **supply, demand, logistics, production, or planning inaccuracies**.  
This whitepaper provides a comprehensive catalog of scenarios with tags to help LLMs classify and explain reasons.

### 1. **Demand Spike Beyond Forecast**
- **Description:** Actual demand far exceeded forecasted dependent demand, exhausting inventory faster than replenishment.  
- **Tag:** `Understock | Demand Spike | Forecast Error`

### 2. **Supplier Delay**
- **Description:** Supplier shipments delayed due to vendor-side capacity issues, logistics disruption, or raw material unavailability.  
- **Tag:** `Understock | Supplier Delay | External Risk`

### 3. **Transportation Delay**
- **Description:** Stock transfer orders (STOs) or purchase orders (POs) delayed in transit due to lead time variability, customs, or logistics bottlenecks.  
- **Tag:** `Understock | Transport Delay | Execution Gap`

### 4. **Production Delay**
- **Description:** Planned production not completed on time due to machine breakdown, labor shortage, or scheduling issues.  
- **Tag:** `Understock | Production Delay | Capacity Constraint`

### 5. **Raw Material Shortage**
- **Description:** Key raw materials unavailable at the plant, preventing finished goods production.  
- **Tag:** `Understock | Raw Material Shortage | Upstream Dependency`

### 6. **Capacity Constraint**
- **Description:** Resource capacity (machines/labor) insufficient to meet the required production volume within the horizon.  
- **Tag:** `Understock | Capacity Constraint | Resource Bottleneck`

### 7. **Long Transportation Lead Time**
- **Description:** Extended transportation lead time between RDC and DC results in projected stock going negative before receipts arrive.  
- **Tag:** `Understock | Long Lead Time | Transport Constraint`

### 8. **Long Production Lead Time**
- **Description:** Extended production lead time prevents timely replenishment at the plant, causing projected stock shortages.  
- **Tag:** `Understock | Long Lead Time | Production Constraint`

### 9. **Network Imbalance**
- **Description:** Stock available upstream but not transferred downstream due to allocation or transport restriction.  
- **Tag:** `Understock | Network Imbalance | Allocation Issue`

### 10. **Frozen Horizon Constraint**
- **Description:** Production or procurement could not be adjusted in the frozen horizon window, leading to temporary shortages.  
- **Tag:** `Understock | Frozen Horizon | Planning Rigidity`

### 11. **Unplanned Outgoing Supply**
- **Description:** Unexpected customer orders or urgent STOs consumed inventory beyond planned levels.  
- **Tag:** `Understock | Unplanned Outgoing | Sudden Demand`

### 12. **Quality Rejections**
- **Description:** Receipts were rejected or blocked due to quality issues, leaving insufficient usable stock.  
- **Tag:** `Understock | Quality Rejection | Operational Issue`

### 13. **Forecast Underestimation**
- **Description:** Forecasted demand was lower than actual, leading to stockouts as receipts were not aligned with reality.  
- **Tag:** `Understock | Forecast Underestimation | Planning Error`

### 14. **Inventory Target Misalignment**
- **Description:** Inventory target not updated for rising demand, causing projected stock to fall short.  
- **Tag:** `Understock | Target Misalignment | SOP Gap`

## Key Observations  
- **Understock drivers:** Demand spikes, supply/transport delays, production bottlenecks, lag week mismatches, capacity shortages.  
- **Overstock drivers:** Over-ordering, early/duplicate receipts, large lot sizes, forecast drops, long lead times with buffers, lag week overforecasting.  
- **Lag Week Impact:** Previous plan mismatches (forecast changes, PO/production delays, over/under forecasting) play a significant role in both understock and overstock.
